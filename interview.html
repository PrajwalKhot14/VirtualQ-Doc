Interview:

For our interviews, we target 4 individuals who accompany their family members to the hospital for regular checkups. We wanted to gain insight on what is possible that they would prefer to do during their wait time. Few questions that we highlighted during the interview were:

  1. Once you have entered the waiting room, how often would you like to be notified about the remaining wait time?
  2. If you are running late for the appointment, given the option, would you swap your appointment with another user who is scheduled after you, with their consent?
  3. Would you prefer to use a text or voice assistant for searching places?
  4. Would you prefer to view search results in multiple formats?

Analysis of Interview

Regarding the frequency of the notifications the one interviewee wanted notifications every time somebody finished their appointment & they were bumped up the queue. Another suggestion for the same question was to have updates at regular intervals of 5 minutes. All interviewees were interested in having an option to swap their slots in case of running late for their appointments. The response for user preference for voice & text search indicated that this choice depended if the user was an expert mobile user who has a history of such searches. Some users who were not exposed to voice interfaces preferred text searches. The responses for the last question were varied, we had suggestions of simple list views as well as locations dropped on a map view.
